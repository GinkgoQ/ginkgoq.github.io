## The limitation of RAG

Retrieval-Augmented Generation has become the default architecture for many AI applications. It improves factuality and reduces hallucination, but it is still only a retrieval layer.

> RAG finds information. It does not understand it. It does not validate it. It does not act on it.

## Beyond retrieval

Real-world systems need more than retrieving documents. They need a pipeline that can turn unstructured information into reliable, actionable intelligence.

<div class="pipeline">
  <span>Ingestion</span>
  <span>→</span>
  <span>Retrieval</span>
  <span>→</span>
  <span>Extraction</span>
  <span>→</span>
  <span>Validation</span>
  <span>→</span>
  <span>Reasoning</span>
  <span>→</span>
  <span>Action</span>
</div>

This pipeline is where domain intelligence emerges. Each step adds a layer of understanding, quality, and context.

## Domain intelligence layer

The domain intelligence layer sits on top of retrieval and turns raw information into decision-ready knowledge.

- Entity and event extraction
- Normalization and canonicalization
- Consistency checks and validation
- Business rules and domain constraints
- Confidence scoring and provenance

This is what makes AI systems reliable in production.
